MINI-REPORT · HEADLINE

Traffic is dropping. Web-form submissions held — and GBP click-throughs convert at ~2× organic.

The conversions on this page are website form-submits only (GA4 form_submit). Direct phone calls and GBP listing actions — the larger mover-lead paths — aren't measured anywhere yet. Read every "GBP CR" / "highest-converting" claim as the form-only slice.

Read this first — three things we've been conflating

When PM dashboards / decks / call notes have said "GBP conversions," "leads from GBP," or "highest-converting channel," they've usually meant one specific slice. There are actually three layers to a GBP lead, and only one is currently in GA4:

  1. GBP listing actions — calls, direction-requests, messages, photo views, AND "Website" button clicks taken on the GBP listing itself. Lives in GBP Insights / Performance API. Never pulled — invisible today.
  2. GBP→site sessions — users who tapped the "Website" button and landed on a GA4-tagged page. Tracked via ?utm_source=gmb&utm_medium=click. ~150/mo last 30d.
  3. GBP→site form-submits — the subset of (2) who submitted a quote form. ~26/mo Q2 2026* partial. This is what every "GBP CR" number on PM dashboards refers to.

The "GBP CR ~17.5%" number is (3) ÷ (2). It is NOT "GBP's overall conversion rate" — that would require dividing all GBP-driven leads (including direct calls) by all GBP listing impressions, neither of which we currently capture. For a moving company, direct phone calls from the GBP listing are typically the dominant lead path. Plan: AN-01 + AN-02 + AN-07 in the P0 lever close all three measurement gaps in M1.

The thesis

Three numbers tell the story. (1) GSC monthly impressions collapsed from 319K (Aug 2025) to 82K (May 2026) — −74%, the result of the Sept 2025 algorithmic refresh wiping the long-tail (435 → 3 keywords at pos 51+). (2) GA4 organic sessions dropped from 709/mo (Q2 2025 peak) to 484/mo (Q1 2026 trough) — −32%, less than half the impression collapse because surviving traffic stayed at the converting end of the funnel. (3) GBP→site form_submit events held flat at 17–21/mo through the cliff and are now growing — Q1 2026 20/mo → Q2 2026* partial 26/mo (+47% est.), with GBP click-throughs submitting forms at 17.5% vs organic 9.2%. On the metric we can measure, the channel never dipped — and is now leading on rate and growing on volume. What we can't yet measure: the calls + directions + messages the GBP listing itself generated (likely the larger slice for movers); the phone-call leads in any channel (CallRail not deployed).

Five readings of the chart

  1. The traffic-vs-web-form-submission divergence is the chart's main story. Organic traffic fell 32% quarterly Q2 2025 → Q1 2026 (709 → 484 sessions/mo). Organic web-form submissions held flat at ~60/mo through Q4 2025 despite traffic dropping — surviving traffic stayed conversion-quality. Then organic form-submits crashed in Q1 2026 (39/mo). GBP→site form-submits never dipped at all — held 17–21/mo throughout the cliff. Traffic dropped; web-form submissions stayed stable and are now growing. (Reminder: phone-call leads — likely the larger slice for movers — are not in this view at all.)
  2. February is the seasonal low for moving in the Detroit metro. Feb 2025: 503 organic sessions / 34 conv. Feb 2026: 471 organic sessions / 31 conv. The Q1 2026 trough partially reflects normal winter seasonality plus the algorithmic cliff layered on top. Q2 typically picks up — Q2 2026* partial recovery is consistent.
  3. GBP wasn't tracked before Feb 2025. Dec 2024 + Jan 2025 GBP = 0 because the ?utm_source=gmb UTM wasn't installed on the GBP listing yet. Pre-engagement GBP traffic was rolling up into Direct.
  4. GBP click-throughs submit forms at ~14–17% vs organic 8–11% — roughly 2× rate on the form-only metric, and rising. The narrow comparison is "of website visitors per channel, what % submitted a quote form": GBP visitors are higher-intent (they tapped a local business listing) and convert more often. This is a real lift, and it's the single strongest measurable data argument for Lever A. Don't overread it as "GBP is the highest-converting channel" overall — the listing-side actions (calls/directions/messages) and the phone-call leads from any channel aren't in this rate at all, and could change the ranking once measured.
  5. As of May 21: 24 GBP→site form_submit events in May 2026 so far — already matching the historical full-month peak (Jul 2025: 24) with 11 days left in the month. Daily pace 1.2/day vs historical 0.63/day. Conservative full-month est. 31 (vs naive linear 37). Plausibly seasonal (Memorial Day) + Lever A early payoff. (Whether the GBP listing also drove proportionally more calls in the same window: unknown — Insights not pulled yet.)

Direct-channel caveat

Direct sessions = 1,448–16,109/mo + 36–111 form_submit/mo. Excluded from the chart for scale reasons. The Sept–Oct 2025 direct spike to 16,109 sessions is suspicious — likely ATB ("Awesome Traffic Bot") artifact. The tool was active in Asana at that time per the Off-Page Risk Assessment §8 audit. Inflates direct counts without proportional conversion lift (Sept direct CR ~1% vs steady-state ~3.3%). Track Direct monthly but treat the 16K spike as artifact, not signal.

LLM channel emerging

LLM-referrer sessions: 0 (Dec 2024) → 17 sessions/mo est. (May 2026). LLM form_submit: 0 → 3 est. Small absolute volume, but the trajectory matters: graduates the Q4 LLM-citation framework from "exploratory" to "warranted." See the LLM Visibility mini-report for the 20-keyword AI-Overview saturation baseline.

What this means for the Q3 plan

Highest leverage

Lever A — Map Presence Expansion. Claiming Detroit Woodward GBP + Rochester GBP compounds the channel where (a) the measurable form-submit rate is highest and (b) the unmeasured listing-side actions (calls, directions, messages) are likely the largest lead source for movers. PM operates 1 of 3 available pins; bringing the other 2 under management roughly doubles the surface area in both layers simultaneously.

Recovery, not rebuild

Web-form submission volume (~190 form_submit/mo combined across channels) never collapsed. The Sept 2025 cliff hit impressions, not customers. Recovery target is +40% impressions M3 (moderate) plus +150% GBP→site form-submits/mo via two new managed pins (aggressive on the form-only metric) — plus establishing baseline visibility on the call layer via CallRail in M1.

P0 closes three gaps

(1) GA4 form_submit fires but isn't flagged — 5-min settings fix. (2) Phone calls untracked — CallRail deployment, owner sign-off. (3) GBP listing actions never pulled — GBP Insights / Performance API monthly. All three are in the P0 mini-report. Until they land, every conversion metric on this page is a partial view of the lead picture.

Reporting reframe

Every monthly report should lead with this divergence chart (sessions + web-form submissions, channel-split) — not GSC clicks alone — and once CallRail + GBP Insights are live, add a stacked-channel "lead picture" view that combines form-submits + calls + listing actions. GSC-only tells the cliff story and misses the recovery. See the GA4 ↔ GSC discrepancy mini-report for the reconciliation framework.
