The Challenges We're Tackling

Organic search — the part that took a hit

−74% impressions on Google Search (Aug 2025 → May 2026)

Google rolled out a core algorithm update in September 2025 that rebalanced rankings on long-tail content. A chunk of your published location pages — primarily templated suburb landing pages — lost their rankings and are no longer attracting search traffic. Monthly impressions went from 319,000 (Aug 2025) to 82,000 (May 2026). Your service pages stayed roughly stable; the damage was concentrated in the suburb-page cohort.

The recovery path: clean up the underperforming suburb pages, deepen the seven service pages that already attract impressions but rank on page 2, and route internal link equity into them. Maps and the GBP channel — the ones that drove your form submissions — were not affected.

Maps reach is limited by geometry

PM operates 3 physical offices but only 1 GBP is under our management

The Walled Lake pin is performing as well as a single pin can — but your declared service area covers cities a single Walled Lake pin physically cannot reach on Maps (Rochester, Royal Oak, Birmingham, Farmington Hills, Plymouth). The Detroit Woodward (1001 Woodward Ave) and Rochester (419 Golf View Ln) offices are listed on your website but their Google Business Profiles are not currently under HQDM management.

Bringing those two pins under management is the single highest-leverage move we can make next quarter. The "detroit moving company" momentum we're already seeing (doubling top-10 reach) gives us a base to build on once a managed Woodward Ave pin comes online.

Measurement gap

Quote-form submissions weren't being counted in Google Analytics

Form submissions on your site fire correctly in Google Analytics (~190 events per month across all traffic sources), but the conversion-event flag in property settings was never enabled — so GA4's native reports showed "0 conversions." We have a 5-minute fix on our side that unlocks accurate conversion reporting going forward.

Beyond that, we'll add two more tracking events to mirror the pattern we use on our other moving accounts: click_phone_number (fires when someone taps a phone-number link on the website) and gmb_click (fires on Google Business Profile-tagged link clicks). Once both are live, monthly reports will show a complete leads picture by channel.

What's Next — The Next 90 Days

Five workstreams running in parallel plus one quick analytics fix. The first one — claiming the unmanaged offices for Maps — is the highest-leverage move; the rest compound it.

Your Detroit and Rochester offices are real, listed on the site, and have already been indexed by Google. The Detroit Woodward pin is the biggest opportunity — you already rank #2-3 average on the Detroit grid from "pin-bleed" without managing the listing. Priority 2

Page surgery — clean up the suburb pages, deepen the service pages

The roughly 70 suburb pages produced before the September 2025 Google update are no longer attracting search traffic — 60+ of them received zero impressions in the last 30 days. We'll redirect or delete about 50 of them (consolidating into the locations that work), upgrade the 7 service pages that have impressions but rank on page 2-3 (hot-tub moving, piano moving, artwork moving, senior moving, local moving, office furniture installation, internal small moving), and build 4 dedicated landing pages for Detroit + Michigan-statewide searches.

Priority 3

Internal linking + schema markup

About 50 specific internal-link changes routing equity from where it currently sits to the pages we want to rank, plus structured-data markup on all upgraded pages so Google can show enhanced results (sitelinks, FAQ snippets, breadcrumb trails). Quiet but compounding work — typically lifts rankings 3-8 positions across the ship-ready keyword band.

Priority 4

Your top blog posts get a lot of impressions but few clicks — we'll refresh them to convert that traffic into commercial inquiries.
