MINI-REPORT · MEASUREMENT

GA4 and GSC tell different stories. Both are right — they measure different things.

A reconciliation framework + trust-matrix for the monthly report.

The thesis

GSC says PM's organic search clicks fell 74% over 12 months. GA4 says PM's Organic Search channel is 2.2–2.7× larger than GSC reports across every time window. Neither is wrong. GSC measures clicks on Google's search results page; GA4 measures sessions on the site, however the user arrived. Four structural gaps account for the divergence — and one of them (GBP click-through) is the reason GBP looks so strong in GA4 but invisible in GSC.

The four structural reasons — in depth

1

GBP click-through (the big one)

GBP's "Click to website" button sends ?utm_source=gmb&utm_medium=click — GA4 buckets these into Organic Search. GSC never sees them because they bypass Google Search results entirely. ~149/mo (30d), ~421/mo (90d), ~543/yr (365d). Fastest-growing slice of GA4 Organic. Every GSC-only report undercounts PM's true converting traffic by 30–40%.

2

Non-Google engines

Bing (50 sess 30d), DuckDuckGo (42), Yahoo (42), MSN (9). ~134 sessions/mo in GA4's "Organic Search" channel that GSC by definition cannot report. Small but real residual traffic source — and the only place where PM's branded queries can compound without Google's local-pack suppression.

3

Sessions ≠ clicks (unit gap)

One GSC click = one Google search-result click. One GA4 session = up to 30 minutes of inactivity, multiple pageviews, can include same-user same-day repeat searches as one session vs N clicks. Most of the residual 1.4–1.5× google/organic gap is this — the multiplier varies by site but is structural.

4

Sampling + bot-filter divergence

GA4 has property-level bot/spam filtering. GSC has its own (independent) heuristics. Neither is documented in full. Small residual gap is unavoidable. Tracking month-over-month change is reliable; absolute cross-platform parity is not. The PM Direct anomaly Sept–Oct 2025 (spike to 16K) is a tell: bot artifact slipped GA4's filter, distorted Direct, did not show in GSC at all.

Recommended monthly report shape

Every monthly report should triangulate. Never quote GSC clicks alone — it understates the converting channel by half. Never quote GA4 Organic Search sessions alone — it conflates Google search with GBP click-through. The format:

  1. Headline chart: GA4 sessions per channel (Organic / GBP / Direct / LLM) + GA4 conversions per channel — split-axis, 18 months.
  2. GSC mini-table: clicks / impressions / position / CTR last 30d vs prior 30d — diagnoses SERP-side ranking only.
  3. Channel attribution panel: Direct + Organic + GBP + LLM + Referral + Paid each as a stack on conv volume + conv rate. GBP must be its own row (utm_source=gmb), not folded into Organic.
  4. Top-mover panel: query-level winners + losers from GSC (rank, clicks); URL-level winners + losers from GA4 (sessions, conv).
  5. Footnote: every report should restate the GSC/GA4 ratio for the current month so future readers understand the unit mismatch isn't a bug.
