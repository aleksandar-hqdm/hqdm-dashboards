What changed in v9 (2026-05-22) · terminology + chart honesty pass

Renamed "Conversions" → "Leads" throughout. The events we count (session_booked, booking_confirmed, contact_form_submission, click_to_dial, click_phone_number, click_to_email) are all lead events — a user started a booking flow, submitted a form, or clicked a phone/email link. They do not represent confirmed paid sessions. Measuring actual customer conversion requires downstream attribution (CallRail call duration + quality, booking-system confirmation feed, payment record) — which is not yet integrated. The dashboard now uses "Leads," "Lead Capture Rate (LCR)," and explicit caveats so the framing is accurate.

Engagement signals removed from the lead chart. In v8 the chart included an amber-dotted "engagement signals" overlay that visually dominated the chart (500+ events vs ~50 leads), even though it was labeled "NOT conversions." That was misleading. Engagement signals (gmb_click + banner_booking_clicks + giftcard_click) are now excluded from the chart entirely and tracked in a separate diagnostic panel on the dashboard for context. None of the YoY, quarterly, or chart numbers include them.

Result (Q1 2025 → Q1 2026 Organic Search): Sessions 8,879 → 10,448 (+18%) · Leads 139 → 164 (+18%) · Booking + form leads only 62 → 110 (+77%) · Lead Capture Rate 1.57% → 1.57% (stable). All numbers exclude the engagement-event artifact. The +18% leads YoY matches the +18% sessions YoY — consistent organic growth tied to LD rank tightening.
