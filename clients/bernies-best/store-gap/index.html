STORE GAP · BIGGEST UNADDRESSED REVENUE LEVER

2.49M monthly SV. 3,338 in-SKU keywords. Effectively zero capture today.

In Bernie's own product categories — digestive, hip/joint, omega/fish-oil, dental, mobility — the store ranks for only 59 keywords total, 75% branded. Truly generic, non-brand rankings: ~6 keywords / ~230 SV/month. This isn't a "lost ground" story. The store never had ground in its own category SERPs. The biggest single opportunity in the engagement.

WHAT

The headline numbers

The store subdomain (store.bernies.com) ranks for 59 keywords total — 53 of them in top-20 — but 44 of 59 (75%) are branded ("Bernie's Perfect Poop", "Charming Chompers", brand misspellings like "betnies"). Of the 15 Ahrefs flags as non-branded, ~7 are "perfect poop" product-name variants (quasi-branded). Truly generic non-brand rankings: ~6 keywords / ~230 SV/month.

Per-PDP: perfect-poop carries 45 of the 59 rankings; charming-chompers and omg-omegas 2-3 each; healthy-hips 1; marvelous-mobility ranks for zero keywords. PDPs are found by people who already know the brand name; they don't show up on bare category SERPs at all.

WHY

Five compounding causes — none are "lost ground"

1

Topical alignment gap

The Perfect Poop PDP's content barely uses category terminology (probiotic, digestive, gut health) in a structured way Google indexes. H1/H2/body don't carry the SKU's actual category keywords. Google doesn't know the PDP is the canonical commercial answer to "dog probiotic" / "fish oil for dogs" / "joint supplement for dogs".

2

Schema gap

PDPs lack Product, Review, FAQ, Organization schema. Native Pet (audited competitor) carries this layer; Bernie's PDPs don't. Schema is approved on PDPs per owner direction (not on articles) — it's a permission-blocked gap, not a strategy disagreement.

3

Subdomain split + weak cross-link

Store lives on store.bernies.com; blog content on www.bernies.com. The blog already ranks for some category terms (digestive, fiber, probiotic) but the authority signal doesn't funnel to the PDP via contextual internal links. The two subdomains are effectively siloed. Addressed by TPL-04 (site-level-upgrades).

4

Parameter pollution dilutes canonical signal

The 2,670 "Crawled - not indexed" pages include parameter-polluted variants of the PDPs themselves (?_gl=, ?utm=, ?_pos=, ?reviews-page=, ?srsltid=). Google sees the PDP as multiple URLs competing with itself. Addressed by Lever 3 (Index Hygiene).

5

5×5 cannibalization pattern (the structural one)

5 products × 5 "Learn More" pages on www.bernies.com directly cannibalize the corresponding store PDPs. The Learn More pages outrank the actual PDP for some queries — sending users to informational content where the buy path is unclear. This pattern is the #1 sitemap action recommended for 12 of 15 mapped top-50 losers (cannibalization + redirect, per top-50-losers).

This isn't a "lost ground" story. The store never had ground in its own category SERPs. PDPs are found by brand search only; they don't surface on bare category terms ("dog probiotic", "joint supplement for dogs", "fish oil for dogs"). That's the 2.49M opportunity universe.

HOW

Detection methodology — 5-step pipeline

Run end-to-end by research/12_store_gap_analysis.py + 14_dedup_gap.py + 40_store_analysis.py:

  1. 1
    Built the SKU keyword universe. For each of Bernie's 5 SKUs, ran DFS keyword_suggestions across 8–15 seed queries per product (e.g. for Perfect Poop: "dog probiotic", "digestive supplement for dogs", "dog gut health", "probiotic chews for dogs", "fiber for dogs", "psyllium for dogs"…). Result: 3,429 unique kws across 5 SKUs.
  2. 2
    Pulled Ahrefs ranked_keywords for both subdomains. www.bernies.com (blog) and store.bernies.com (commerce) separately. GSC is blind on the store subdomain (data gap from day one — store.bernies.com is NOT in GSC), so DFS + Ahrefs are the only signals. DFS supplemented as third source for keyword-level rank validation.
  3. 3
    Classified each kw into one of three states. PURE_OPPORTUNITY = no Bernie's URL ranks ≤100 anywhere (commercial demand exists, Bernie's is invisible). WRONG_URL = a non-store URL (blog / KB / University) ranks ≤30 but the corresponding store PDP doesn't (authority on wrong page type for commercial intent). STORE_GOOD = store PDP ranks ≤20 (working as intended).
  4. 4
    Deduplicated cross-SKU overlap. Many kws (fiber, probiotic, digestive) appear in multiple SKU universes. Deduplication assigns each kw to its single best-fit SKU (closest topical match). Reduces 3,429 → 3,338 unique opportunity kws.
  5. 5
    Validated SERP intent on the top opportunity kws. Manual DFS SERP pulls on the 60 highest-SV kws to filter out wrong-intent (e.g. "dog probiotic" returns commercial-intent SERPs → keep; "best dog food" returns informational/listicle SERPs → flag as informational, redirect to blog content).
Final picture per SKU: Perfect Poop 1,011 PURE_OPP kws / 1.38M SV · Healthy Hips 1,001 PURE_OPP / 444K · OMG Omegas 622 PURE_OPP / 426K · Charming Chompers 678 PURE_OPP / 244K · Marvelous Mobility 26 PURE_OPP / 710. Plus 15 WRONG_URL kws where authority sits on a blog/KB/University page instead of the PDP.

What this drives — Lever 2 (Store PDP build-out)

PDPs need: (1) topical content aligned with category kws (TPL-04 wires up blog→PDP authority); (2) Product/Review/FAQ/Organization schema; (3) internal links from existing blog content (TPL-03, TPL-04 in site-level-upgrades); (4) parameter pollution canonicalized (Lever 3); (5) Learn More pages 301-redirected to canonical PDP (12 cannibalization fixes from top-50-losers).

Capturing even 1% of 2.49M monthly SV = 25K monthly visits = 10× the store's current organic traffic. The Charming Chompers Q1 pattern (+69% sessions, +340% transactions in the Q1 YoY winners table) shows the upside is real once the PDP is properly aligned.
