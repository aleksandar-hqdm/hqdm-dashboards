Methodology

  • Pair selection. 20 queries where Spot & Tango ranks in top-15 and Bernie's ranks below 15. Pairs span Bernie's Perfect Poop SKU cluster (digestive, stool quality, bowel) plus adjacent dog-health topics.
  • Fetch. All 40 URLs captured as rendered; redirects followed and noted.
  • Parse. Parsed with chrome-stripping (script, style, nav, header, footer, aside, form, any display:none / aria-hidden=true removed). Widget classes (related posts, sidebars, share buttons, breadcrumbs, author bios, product carousels) stripped.
  • Structure extraction. Each article parsed into ordered sections defined by H1/H2/H3/H4 boundaries. Record: heading text, level, paragraph count, bullet count, paragraph words, list words, total words.
  • Semantic classification. Every heading classified — declarative, numbered_list_item, question, how_to, FAQ_or_conclusion.
  • Content-gap detection. Five gap types — STUB (heading <50w body), EMPTY_SECTION (heading no body), BARE_LIST (bullets only), LIST_HEAVY (lists dominate), THIN_H2 (single-paragraph H2 no sub-structure).

The dominant signal — numbered H3 enumeration

S&T uses 219 numbered H3 headings across the 20-article cohort (avg 11 per article). Bernie's uses 4 total — a 55× delta. This is the single largest measured signal and the most plausible explanation for the AI Overview citation gap. Numbered H3 sub-sections (25–150w each) are deliberately compact to function as snippet / AIO answer units. Bernie's articles list the same parallel items as bold-label paragraphs or paragraph-internal bullets, which don't register as discrete anchor units.

The verified recipe — apply to every M1/M2 refresh

Structural skeleton (mandatory)
  • Word count: 2,100–3,500. Most Bernie's articles at ~1,500-1,700 — additive depth, not trimming. 6 pairs need ≥3× expansion.
  • Heading depth: 6–10 H2 anchor sections + 15–25 H3 sub-sections. Bernie's avg 8.2 H3 today; target 18.
  • Numbered enumeration: every parallel list (causes, symptoms, treatments, prevention) becomes numbered H3s, not bold-inline labels.
  • Introductions before lists: 30-60w paragraph framing why the list exists.
  • Brand byline + visible publish + 'Updated' date (TPL-01 surfaces dateModified).
  • "Final Thoughts" conclusion — most consistent gainer element (10 of 12 in prior audit).
  • FAQ section (3-5 Q&A) as 2nd-to-last H2 — recommended, not mandatory.
  • Product mentions: 5-8 inline + sidebar Related Products. Bernie's current ~36 to consolidate.
Bernie's E-E-A-T overlay (add what S&T omits)
  • "Reviewed by Dr. X, DVM" header note (AnimalBiome pattern). S&T top-gainers deliberately omit DVM citation.
  • Body-cited vet when making clinical claims. Bernie's already has named-vet content — surface it.
  • Cite Cornell, NIH/PubMed, Merck Vet Manual, AAFCO on YMYL claims (AnimalBiome + Native Pet pattern).
  • Tone: informational + named-vet attribution. Avoid S&T/Wopet's fear-urgency framing — Bernie's brand can't carry it without credibility cost.

Net pattern: S&T structural skeleton + AnimalBiome/Native Pet E-E-A-T overlay. Differentiate by adding what S&T's winning articles deliberately omit, while preserving the structural elements the top SERPs reward.

Net takeaway

The pattern is not opinion — it's measured across 20 head-to-head matches. Bernie's averages 36% of S&T's H3 count, uses 4 numbered-enumeration headings vs S&T's 219, and ships ~5 footer-template H3 pollution units per article. Bernie's covers the same topical material at ~40% of S&T's body length on average.

The fix is template-level + per-article restructuring, not full rewrites. Bernie's content is qualitatively fine. Bernie's structure is leaving most of the SEO value on the table — particularly the AI Overview / Featured Snippet extraction opportunity that S&T's numbered H3 enumeration captures. The M1 sprint should target 2 template changes (site-level-upgrades) + 4 P0 article restructures (above), not 20 rewrites.
