The Big Picture

You're the #1-ranked moving and storage company on Google Maps in Santa Clarita by every measure we track. Out of 350 spots on the map where we checked who shows up for three top searches (local movers, moving company near me, packing & storage), AQMS appears in the top 3 results in 224 of them — more than any other Santa Clarita mover, and more than the next two competitors combined. Your 4.9-star rating and 452 reviews (up from 441 when we started in March) keep your reputation lead solid too.

The website tells a different story. Search traffic to your site collapsed in January — organic search visitors went from ~400/month at the end of 2025 down to 75 in February 2026 (the low point). This was almost certainly an algorithm update combined with some other channel changes that pre-dated our engagement. Since we started in March, the recovery has been steep: 109 sessions in March, 320 in April, 337 in May (and counting). The line is going back up.

The most important number: even though traffic is down, your conversion rate is actually UP year-over-year. In Q2 2025 (baseline), 10.73% of search visitors became leads. So far in Q2 2026, that's 11.26% — slightly higher. The visitors you're getting now are better-quality, not worse. That's the foundation for the next 90 days.

What We're Working On — Next 90 Days

Priority 1

Defend your #1 Santa Clarita Maps position

You're already #1, so this is about keeping what you have while a couple of competitors push. Four things: (1) refreshing and optimizing your Google Business profile (photos, attributes, products, description), (2) doubling post cadence to 2x/month, (3) a review-acquisition campaign so your ~4 new reviews/month becomes 6–8 (post-job SMS request + invoice review link), and (4) strengthening the /santa-clarita/ page on your website (52K impressions/90 days but averaging position 20 — should be top 10).

Priority 2

Accelerate the website-traffic recovery

Your organic traffic has tripled since February — the recovery is real. We're going to (a) verify all conversion-tracking events are firing correctly in Google Analytics (already confirmed: form submits, phone-number clicks, and Google Business clicks are all tracked), (b) investigate the May impression cliff (May 2026 is showing 10× fewer search impressions than April — could be AI-Overview encroachment, data lag, or something else; we're digging in), (c) do a backlink audit to find and disavow any spam links that may have contributed to the January cliff, and (d) refresh your top blog posts with commercial calls-to-action so traffic converts harder.

Priority 3

Strengthen your multi-location SoCal pages

You already have city pages for Santa Clarita, San Diego, Burbank, Encinitas, La Jolla, and Temecula — but they're underperforming. /san-diego/ is averaging position 15; /san-diego/packing-unpacking-services/ is at position 41. These are wasted assets. We're going to rebuild each location page with full depth (local pricing, neighborhood callouts, testimonials, schema markup) and add new ones for the 5 outer-cities your single GBP pin doesn't reach (Castaic, Simi Valley, San Fernando, Moorpark, Agua Dulce).

Priority 4

Capitalize on packing & storage momentum

Packing & storage is the one search you're actively gaining on — up almost a full position in 2.5 months. Storage is also recurring revenue, which is structurally more valuable than one-time moving jobs. We'll build a dedicated /storage-units-santa-clarita/ page (full pricing + unit-size matrix), a dedicated /packing-services-santa-clarita/ page, publish two blog posts in May–June that link back to them, and add monthly Google posts specifically about storage and packing.

Priority 5

Show up in Google's AI Overviews

Google is increasingly answering "how-to" and "how much" questions with AI Overviews (those summary boxes at the top of search results). We checked 20 priority searches for you — 4 of them now have AI Overviews, and AQMS isn't cited in any of them. We'll refresh your existing how-to blog content (e.g., "How to choose the right moving company") and add 3 new ones (cost calculators, packing guides) optimized for AI Overview citation — FAQ format, expert quotes, video embeds. Getting even one citation here is a meaningful brand signal.
