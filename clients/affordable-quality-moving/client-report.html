What Happened — A Timeline

Before HQDM · Oct–Dec 2025
Direct-traffic spike

Sessions labeled as "Direct" surged from ~450/month to 1,901 in November. This is the signature of a third-party traffic-tool — not real visitors. (Common across the industry; we've seen the same pattern on other moving accounts.) It was inflating the numbers but not contributing real leads.

January 2026 · HQDM engagement begins
The cliff hits — Direct & Organic both crash

The traffic-tool source stopped feeding the site (Direct went from 1,288 to 54 in one month, −96%). At the same time, a Google algorithm update appears to have rebalanced rankings on a chunk of your long-tail pages. Organic search sessions fell from 400 in December to 97 in January, then to 75 in February.

February–March 2026 · Stabilization
We stopped the bleeding

We made sure none of the old vendor's automated traffic/link campaigns were still running, audited the site for any signals that may have flagged it, and refocused effort onto the channels that survived: Google Maps and your Google Business profile (both of which were at full strength).

April–May 2026 · Recovery
Organic search comes back hard; form submissions recover across channels

Organic sessions in April jumped to 320 (+193% vs March), May is at 337 and counting — last 90 days are up +34%. Form submissions across all channels totalled 86 over Apr + May (39 + 47, including the dev-team Flamingo backfill that recovered the Apr 14 → May 28 tracking gap). Maps held #1 throughout and extended the lead (+46 top-3 placements since March).

What hurt you (Q4 2025)

The "fake Direct" spike

"Direct" sessions (people typing your URL or arriving with no referrer) ballooned from ~450/month to 1,901 in November — and then crashed to 54 in January. The engagement rate on those sessions was abnormally low (~3% on the "(not set)" landing page = bot footprint). This was almost certainly a third-party "traffic tool" or link-building automation running before our engagement. It inflated your numbers but contributed no real leads, and when it stopped, the algorithm appears to have rebalanced rankings shortly after.

Heads-up: a chunk of "Direct" traffic is actually job applicants hitting /careers/ — HR traffic, not customer enquiries. Real customer Direct sessions are about 600/month now and growing.
