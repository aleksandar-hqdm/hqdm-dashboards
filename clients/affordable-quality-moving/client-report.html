The Big Picture

You're the #1-ranked moving and storage company on Google Maps in Santa Clarita by every measure we track. Out of 350 spots on the map where we checked who shows up for the three most important searches (local movers, moving company near me, and packing & storage), AQMS appears in the top 3 results in 224 of them — more than any other Santa Clarita mover, and more than the next two competitors combined. Your 4.9-star rating and 452 reviews (up from 441 when we started in March) keep your reputation lead solid too.

The honest read on the last 2.5 months: you're holding the lead, not extending it. Average rank is essentially flat (3.67 → 3.60 — a slight improvement), but you've lost 15 of those "top 3" map spots, mostly on the local movers search. Meanwhile, a competitor — Camelot Moving & Storage — has aggressively added 47 new top-3 spots in the same window. The plan for the next 90 days is built around that reality: defend the #1 position, double down on the search where you're actually gaining (packing & storage), and reach into the surrounding cities your current pin doesn't cover.

What We're Working On — Next 90 Days

Priority 1

Defend your #1 Santa Clarita Maps position

You're already #1, so this is about keeping what you have while a couple of competitors push. We're focused on four things: (1) refreshing and optimizing your Google Business profile (photos, attributes, products, the description), (2) doubling your post cadence to 2x/month (one informational, one offer), (3) a review-acquisition campaign so the ~4 new reviews/month you're already getting becomes 6–8 (we'll send post-job SMS requests and add a Google review link to your invoices), and (4) strengthening the /local-movers/ and /moving-company-near-me/ pages on your website — these are the search results you lost ground on.

Priority 2

Capitalize on the packing & storage momentum

The packing & storage search is the one you're actively gaining on — up almost a full position in 2.5 months. Storage is also recurring revenue, which is structurally more valuable than one-time moving jobs. We're going to build two dedicated landing pages on your website (one for storage units with full pricing + unit-size matrix, one for packing services with the full tier breakdown), publish two blog posts in May–June that link back to them ("How much storage do you need?" + "Is full-packing worth it?"), and add monthly Google posts specifically about storage and packing (separate from your moving posts).

Priority 3

Reach into the cities your current pin doesn't cover

Your Google Business pin is in Santa Clarita, and it reaches confidently for about 5 miles around it. But you serve 20 cities — including Castaic, Simi Valley, San Fernando, Moorpark, and Agua Dulce — that are further out than the pin's reach. When someone in Simi Valley searches "movers near me," your competitors there show up, not you. We're going to build dedicated service-area pages for the five most strategic outer cities (with local references, testimonials, and proper schema markup) so the website ranks for those city-specific searches, and we'll set up Driving Direction pins on your Google profile to send signals about those areas.
