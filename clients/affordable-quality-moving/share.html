May 2026 Update · Months 4–5 of our work together (engagement started January 2026)

The Headline

When we started in January, your website was in the middle of a steep drop — organic search traffic had just fallen from 400 sessions in December to 75 in February (an 81% cliff). Over the four months since, we've turned that around. As of May, organic sessions are back at 337/month — 4.5 times the February low, and the last two months alone are up +134% in traffic and +164% in leads versus the first quarter. The conversion rate is also higher than it was a year ago (11.26% vs 10.73%). Your Maps position never broke — still #1 in Santa Clarita by every measure we track.

What Happened — A Timeline

Before HQDM · Oct–Dec 2025
Direct-traffic spike

Sessions labeled as "Direct" surged from ~450/month to 1,901 in November. This is the signature of a third-party traffic-tool — not real visitors. (Common across the industry; we've seen the same pattern on other moving accounts.) It was inflating the numbers but not contributing real leads.

January 2026 · HQDM engagement begins
The cliff hits — Direct & Organic both crash

The traffic-tool source stopped feeding the site (Direct went from 1,288 to 54 in one month, −96%). At the same time, a Google algorithm update appears to have rebalanced rankings on a chunk of your long-tail pages. Organic search sessions fell from 400 in December to 97 in January, then to 75 in February.

February–March 2026 · Stabilization
We stopped the bleeding

We made sure none of the old vendor's automated traffic/link campaigns were still running, audited the site for any signals that may have flagged it, and refocused effort onto the channels that survived: Google Maps and your Google Business profile (both of which were at full strength).

April–May 2026 · Recovery
Organic + leads both come back hard

Organic sessions in April jumped to 320 (+193% vs March), May is at 337 and counting. Leads/conversions are up +164% versus Q1. Most importantly, the quality of those visitors is better than the previous year — your conversion rate is up 13%.

What's Next — The Next 90 Days

Five priorities for the next quarter. The first two compound the recovery curve you're already on; the last three open up new growth.

Priority 1

Defend your #1 Santa Clarita Maps position

You're already #1, so this is about keeping what you have while Camelot pushes hard. Four things: refresh and optimize your Google Business profile (photos every 30 days, attributes, description), double post cadence to 2x/month, launch a review-acquisition workflow targeting +20 reviews by August, and strengthen the /santa-clarita/ page on your website (52K impressions/90d but averaging position 20 — we want it top 10).

Priority 2

Compound the organic recovery

The recovery curve is real — we want to keep it accelerating. We'll do a full backlink audit to find and disavow any spam links that may still be dragging on the site after the January cliff, refresh your top blog posts with stronger calls-to-action, and investigate the May impression drop in Google Search Console (still under review — could be data lag, could be AI Overview encroachment on long-tail pages). Goal: organic sessions reach 500+/month by August.

Priority 3

Strengthen your multi-location SoCal pages

You already have city pages for Santa Clarita, San Diego, Burbank, Encinitas, La Jolla, and Temecula — but most are underperforming. /san-diego/ averages position 15; /san-diego/packing/ averages position 41. We're going to rebuild each with full depth (local pricing, neighborhood callouts, testimonials, schema markup) and add new pages for the 5 outer cities your Santa Clarita GBP pin doesn't reach (Castaic, Simi Valley, San Fernando, Moorpark, Agua Dulce).

Priority 4

Capitalize on packing & storage momentum

Packing & storage is the one search you're actively gaining on — up almost a full position in 2.5 months. Storage is also recurring revenue (different economics from one-time moves). We'll build a dedicated /storage-units-santa-clarita/ page (full pricing + unit-size matrix), a dedicated /packing-services-santa-clarita/ page, publish two utility blog posts that link back to them, and add monthly Google posts specifically about storage and packing.

Priority 5

Get cited in Google's AI Overviews

Google is increasingly answering "how-to" and "how much" questions with AI Overviews (those summary boxes at the top of results). We checked 20 priority searches — 4 already have AI Overviews, and AQMS isn't cited in any of them. We'll refresh your existing how-to blog content (e.g., "How to choose the right moving company") and add 3 new ones (a Santa Clarita moving-cost calculator, packing guides) formatted for AI Overview citation (FAQ schema, expert quotes, video embeds). Even one citation here is a meaningful brand-trust signal.
